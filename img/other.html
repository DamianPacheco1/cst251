History of Animal Pictures

The first animal picture can be dated between April and July of 1842. The image was taken by a french photographer named Joseph-Philibert Girault de Prangey and depicts a cow laying down and resting next to a cart at a cattle market. Animal photography became more popular after National Geographic published their first photos in their magazine. The pictures shown were of deer taken by a tripwire camera system.

Why Take Pictures of Animals?

Whether it's taking pictures of your pet or of wild animals, animal photography is important. Animal photography is important as you’re able to document an animal or species forever, letting future generations see an animal that could have potentially gone extinct. Animal Pictures also can be shared all around the world, letting people who wouldn’t be able to see that animal see that animal without having to travel around the world. Animal Photography also contributes to conservation efforts and overall awareness of animals that are threatened or are being affected negatively by humans. For example, pictures of plastics in the ocean affecting turtles.

What Do You Need for Animal Pictures?

All you need for animal photography is a camera and patience. But to take high quality pictures you would need photography skills, knowledge of the surrounding area and the species you're trying to photograph, and different lenses for smaller animals like ants.
